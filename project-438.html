Project Brief

In this academic project for a senior-level experience design course, teams were tasked with solving a problem for a client through experience design, in which our team chose Kia.

My main roles were conducting user research, scripting user scenarios, prototyping the dealership inventory page, and organizing our weekly slide deck presentations to effectively communicate our designs.

The Client

Who is Kia?

Kia is a South Korean automotive manufacturer known for offering vehicles with abundant features and technology at competitive pricing, making them attractive options for budget-conscious consumers.

Problem Space

1st in vehicle dependency, last in customer satisfaction

Despite ranking highest among mass market brands in vehicle dependency, Kia ranked last in customer satisfaction at dealerships, indicating a significant opportunity for improvement through experience design.

Problem Statement

Kia customers encounter uncertainty and transparency issues on vehicle pricing between the online research process and in-person dealership interactions, leading to lack of trust among customers.

After conducting and analyzing secondary research, we found that Kia customers encounter uncertainty and transparency issues on vehicle pricing between the online research process and in-person dealership interactions, leading to lack of trust among customers.

Problem Framing

How might we facilitate a seamless transition between the online and in-person purchasing experience to expedite and enable informed decisions for Kia buyers?

Solution

Extending Kias website on desktop to improve informed decision making and allow smooth transition when moving from online to in-person.

The design solution intervenes throughout the customer's pre-purchasing journey and achieves two goals:

1) Improving informed decision making and transparency for customers online by providing more readily available information.

2) Bridging the online to in-person experience through expanding the user profile to include the pre-purchasing process and become accessible by dealership employees.

User Testing Method

Who

  • 6 participants: 2 current Kia owners and 4 people currently looking to purchase a new vehicle

    • What

  • With the think-aloud method, participants were given a set of typical customer tasks and asked to go through the website while verbalizing their thoughts.
  • A post-test survey and short-interview were combined with A/B testing to compare participants' evaluation of the current Kia website compared to our prototype

    • Why

  • We wanted to understand which parts of Kia's current website were working and which could be improved to better support informed decision making and improve efficiency in the transition from their online to in-person purchasing journey.

    • Day 4: Analyzing User Testing Data

    Three main insights were gathered from user testing to validate effective areas of our prototype and inform improvements for future iterations:

    1) Users thought our prototype provided better informed decision making compared to Kias existing website.

    2) Users liked the direct comparison features between vehicle builds and dealership offers in the new prototype (but stakeholders have concerns around how this will affect dealerships.)

    3) Inconsistent design system led to confusion and slowed the purchasing process for users.

    Prototype

    Final Design

    Key Design Decisions (1/6)

    Customizing Build

    We improved the original customizing build page by improving the visibility of essential information in the decision making process, such as pricing options, providing feature explanations, and more visual information to improve efficiency of informed decision making for customers.

    Key Design Decisions (2/6)

    Vehicle Build and Price Comparison

    A new feature allowing customers to compare two vehicle builds directly without opening multiple tabs, with a side by side price comparison of features to support more informed decision making.

    Key Design Decisions (3/6)

    Checking and Comparing Inventory

    The existing website only shows if similar vehicles are in stock. Therefore, we added a dedicated dealership offer page for direct comparison of inventory prices across Canadian dealerships, aligning Kia with top competitors like Buick, Chevrolet, and Mitsubishi, known for strong vehicle dependency and customer satisfaction rankings.

    Key Design Decisions (4/6)

    User Profile to Streamline Pre-purchasing Experience

    Kia's current user profile is limited to the post-purchasing experience so we integrated the pre-purchasing process to help bridge the online to in-person experience.

    Key Design Decisions (5/6)

    Pre-fill Documents to Reduce In-person Wait Times

    To further bridge the online to in-person experience, we added the ability to pre-fill required documents such as proof of insurance online, which are normally done in person, to reduce wait times in person and expedite the purchasing process.

    Key Design Decisions (6/6)

    Integrating User Profile with Employee Interface

    We adapted the Activix interface used by Kia employees to include access to customers' user profile information, to facilitate smoother interactions between employees and customers in the dealership.

    Value Proposition

    What value does our design deliver?

    For customers, the intervention reduces cognitive overhead in the purchasing process and enhances transparency between online and in-person interactions, ensuring customers feel confident in making an informed decision for their ideal vehicle. For Kia, increased customer trust and satisfaction increases the probability of purchase completions and also future purchases from customers.

    Final Takeaways

    What I learned...

    1) You don't need to start off as an expert in the domain you're designing in.

    The benefits of choosing a topic completely unfamiliar to us was that the team was able to truly practice user-centred design thinking in empathizing and understanding their needs.

    2) User testing helps overcome false assumptions.

    Through multiple rounds of user testing, we were able recognize and correct many of our assumptions, and reframe our problem statement.
