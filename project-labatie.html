Project Brief

In this academic project, teams were tasked with creating a visual identity through graphic assets and a microsite for a client. Our team was assigned La B√¢tie Festival de Geneve.

Who are La B√¢tie Festival de Geneve?

An 18-day multidisclipinary festival hosted primarily in Gevene, Switzerland with venues all around Geneve and neighbouring cities, featuring artists well-known and emerging, local and international.

Problem

Organizers and artists work tirelessly to hold the annual 18-day La B√¢tie Festival de Geneve, but there is no way for customers to stay engaged with La Batie when the festival is not running.

The design solution consists of two parts:

La B√¢tie's magnitude. It is on its 47th year and is an 18-days long which is more than most performance festivals. Its multidisciplinary nature means there are almost 20 categories of creative genres and over 100 artists (based on 2023).

Art Direction

Translating Graphic Design to Visual Design in Microsite

To summarize our art direction rationale:

  • san-serif and serif font combination to balance tradition and modernity
  • monochromatic colour scheme so the central focus of the site would be on artist content
  • use of grid for cohesive and structured content organization across site
  • full-bleed multimedia to create immersive experience focused on uncovering artist information

    • Reflection

    What I learned

    Decisions should help surface best work rather than be diplomatic.

    When working in a team, I learned that even though my work may not be used in the final product, it is equally valuable in the iterative process to increase our opportunity for success.

    Wide explorations early on allow for the best content to surface.

    The team created hundreds of iterations to make sure that we explored every possible solution because each week's decisions are cumulative and when we iterate a lot we are able to carry over the best work into the next week, and the weeks after.

    Things that can be improved

    Due to the project's scope and time limitations, we couldn't carry out usability testing to validate our design. However, conducting usability testing would be my next step to further develop this project.
