Project Brief

In this academic project, teams were tasked with creating a visual identity through graphic assets and a microsite for a client. Our team was assigned La Bâtie Festival de Geneve.

The Client

Who are La Bâtie Festival de Genève?

An 18-day multidisclipinary festival hosted primarily in Gevène, Switzerland with venues all around Geneve and neighbouring cities, featuring artists well-known and emerging, local and international.

Problem

There is no way to stay engaged with La Bâtie between their annual festivals.

Organizers and artists work tirelessly to hold the annual 18-day La Bâtie Festival de Genève, but there is no way for customers to stay engaged with La Batie when the festival is not running.

Solution

Design pre-event graphic assets a post-event microsite to sustain interest between festivals.

The design solution consists of two parts:

1) pre-event graphic assets in the form of promotional posters placed around relevant areas in Genève and merchandise for purchase before and during festival to get create anticipation prior to the festival.

2) a post-festival microsite offering an intimate glimpse into artists’ creative processes and personal passions. Facilitating deeper connections, it bridges the gap between fans and artists, ensuring to forge a more personal relationship between them, and sustaining engagement beyond the festival.

Secondary Research

What distinguishes La Bâtie from other festivals?

La Bâtie Festival de Geneve is on its 47th year and is an 18-days long which is more than most performance festivals. Its multidisciplinary nature means there are almost 20 categories of creative genres and over 100 artists (in 2023).

The Problem

For the amount of hard work from artists, all year round to put on a festival of this size, audiences are only able to appreciate its work for the 18-days duration, and even then the festival goers are likely not attending all 18-days.

Design Intervention

A pre-event and post-event design intervention can help audiences engage with La Batie outside the time and space of the actual occuring festival.

Translating Graphic Design to Visual Design in Microsite

There were a few considerations made when transferring the graphic design to visual. We focused on keeping our art direction, but the grid gutter was made smaller to accommodate for the information being highlighted on the microsite pages. Furthermore, content had to be placed strategically to allow for smooth interaction transitions.

    Reflection

    Things I learned...

    1) In teamwork, prioritize the best work over diplomacy.

    When working in a team, I learned that even though my work may not be used in the final product, it is equally valuable in the iterative process to increase our opportunity for success.

    2) Wide explorations early on leads to optimal results.

    The teaching team was impressed with our final product and I think one of the crucial factors in our success was that we generated hundreds of iterations, leveraging cumulative decisions to carry over the best work into later phases of the project.

    3) Let go of emotional attachments.

    I created work that I was really proud of and even if it wasn't used in the final product, and I have (mostly) learned to accept it and keep moving forward.
